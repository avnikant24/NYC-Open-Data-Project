Brief Findings of Charts

Fur: This database shows that the primary fur color for squirrels is divided into 3 key colors: gray, cinnamon, and black. From the charts, it is found that 83.8% of squirrels are gray-colored, 12.8% are cinnamon-colored, and only 3.5% are black-colored. Numerically, the bar graph shows that 825 of founded squrriels were gray, 126 founded were cinnamon, and 34 were black. Overall, the majority fur color was gray for squirrels that were found in Central Park.

Location: This database is split into 2 locations where squirrels can be found: above ground and ground plane. Percentage wise, 72.8% of squirrels were found on ground plane and 27.2% were found above ground. In whole numbers that means, 713 squirrels were found on ground plane whereas 267 squirrels were found above ground. This encapsulates the ground plane location is where most squirrels are found.
